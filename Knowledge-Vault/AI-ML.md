# Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning
This section provides an overview of core research papers, engineering practices, and system architectures that define modern AI systems.

---

## Reasoning and Prompting

### Chain of Thought (CoT) Prompting
- A prompting method that improves reasoning by having models generate intermediate logical steps before producing an answer.
- Increases accuracy, interpretability, and performance on tasks involving math, planning, or multi-step logic.

---

## Reinforcement Learning Foundations

### RL with Verifiable Rewards
- A reinforcement learning approach that uses objective and verifiable reward signals.
- Reduces reward hacking and strengthens alignment with correct behavior.

### RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback)
- The training methodology used for many large language models.
- Models learn from human preference rankings, producing safer and more natural responses.

### Atari DQN (Deep Q Network)
- A landmark RL model from 2013 to 2015 that learned to play Atari games directly from raw pixels.
- Established deep reinforcement learning as a scalable and practical method.

---

## Neural Network Architecture and Optimization

### Dropout (Hinton)
- A regularization technique that randomly drops neurons during training.
- Prevents co-adaptation, improves generalization, and reduces overfitting.

### AlexNet and ImageNet (2012)
- A breakthrough convolutional neural network demonstrating the power of deep learning in computer vision.
- Introduced GPU training, ReLU activations, and deeper convolutional layers.

### Retentive Networks
- A modern sequence modeling architecture proposed as an alternative to transformers.
- Enables longer context windows with improved efficiency and reduced compute cost.

## Reliability, Grounding, and Safety

### Hallucination Research
- Research into why models produce incorrect or fabricated outputs.
- Focus areas include uncertainty, grounding, retrieval augmentation, calibrated reasoning, and safety techniques.

## AI Engineering and Infrastructure

### AI Engineering by Chip Huyen
- A comprehensive guide to real-world machine learning production systems.
- Covers deployment patterns, monitoring, drift detection, real-time inference, data pipelines, and lifecycle management.

### Fundamentals of Data Engineering
- Foundational material on ingestion, storage, modeling, transformation, orchestration, and serving.
- Defines the infrastructure that supports modern AI systems.

### Algorithms for AI
- A survey covering both classical and modern algorithms.
- Includes search methods, heuristics, optimization, gradient descent, neural networks, and reinforcement learning.

### AI Student Handbook
- Broad introduction to machine learning concepts, math foundations, ethics, and research practices.

## Agentic Systems Architecture

### Agentic Systems (Autonomous and Multi-Step Agents)
- A design pattern for building AI agents capable of planning, reasoning, and executing multi-step actions.
- Key components include goal decomposition, memory systems, tool use, action loops, environment interaction, safety layers, and multi-agent coordination.
