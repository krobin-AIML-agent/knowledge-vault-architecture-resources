# Psychology, Criminology, and Social Dynamics

This section reflects how I understand human behavior, motivation, social systems, and the structural factors that influence outcomes across people and communities. The goal is to connect individual psychology with broader social and institutional dynamics in a clear and coherent way.

## Introductory Psychology Foundational Theories

* Covers core theories that explain how people learn, think, feel, and behave including motivation, conditioning, reinforcement, cognition, memory, and perception.
* Highlights how environment, upbringing, and mental frameworks shape decision making and performance.
* Establishes foundational concepts from social psychology and industrial-organizational psychology that directly translate into leadership, teamwork, and communication.

**Books**:
- [*Psychology*](https://openstax.org/details/books/psychology-2e) by OpenStax (2e) - Free open-source textbook
- [*Psychology: Themes and Variations*](https://www.cengage.com/c/psychology-themes-and-variations-10e-weiten/) by Wayne Weiten (10th Edition)

## Introduction to Criminology Hagan and Daigle

* Explains major theories of crime including biological, psychological, sociological, and environmental influences.
* Breaks down research methods used to study crime patterns, behavior, and justice systems.
* Reinforces the idea that crime is shaped by social structures, opportunity, inequality, and systemic factors rather than isolated individual choices.

**Book**: [*Introduction to Criminology: Theories, Methods, and Criminal Behavior*](https://www.sagepub.com/en-us/nam/introduction-to-criminology/book262122) by Frank E. Hagan & Leah E. Daigle (10th Edition)

## His Name Is George Floyd

* Provides a detailed account of how one person's life was shaped by generational poverty, systemic inequities, policing practices, and structural barriers.
* Highlights how institutions, policies, and social context shape outcomes far beyond individual actions.
* Reinforces the connection between lived experience, community conditions, and broader patterns of justice and injustice.

**Book**: [*His Name Is George Floyd: One Man's Life and the Struggle for Racial Justice*](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/670217/his-name-is-george-floyd-by-robert-samuels-and-toluse-olorunnipa/) by Robert Samuels & Toluse Olorunnipa

## The Color of Courage

* Examines how Black and Brown professionals navigate corporate systems that often carry hidden and overt barriers.
* Focuses on workplace dynamics, power structures, representation, and the emotional labor tied to navigating inequity.
* Makes clear how organizational culture, leadership behavior, and internal processes shape the experience of marginalized employees.

**Book**: [*The Memo: What Women of Color Need to Know to Secure a Seat at the Table*](https://www.hachettebookgroup.com/titles/minda-harts/the-memo/9780316451659/) by Minda Harts (similar themes) - *Note: "The Color of Courage" may refer to academic papers or organizational studies on race in corporate America*

## Black Male Students and the School to Prison Pipeline

* Explores how disciplinary policies, tracking, resource disparities, and biased expectations funnel Black boys into punitive systems instead of educational pathways.
* Highlights the structural alignment between school discipline practices and criminal justice outcomes.
* Reinforces the connection between early experiences, institutional design, and long-term social mobility.

**Books**:
- [*Pushout: The Criminalization of Black Girls in Schools*](https://thenewpress.com/books/pushout) by Monique W. Morris
- [*The New Jim Crow: Mass Incarceration in the Age of Colorblindness*](https://thenewpress.com/books/new-jim-crow) by Michelle Alexander
- Academic research on the school-to-prison pipeline from educational justice organizations

## Summary

* These notes connect individual psychology, criminal justice systems, and social structures into a unified view of how behavior, opportunity, and outcomes are shaped.
* The central theme is that individual actions cannot be understood separately from institutional design, historical context, and systemic patterns.
* Together, these materials create a framework for understanding how environments, policies, and power structures influence human development, workplace dynamics, and social mobility.
